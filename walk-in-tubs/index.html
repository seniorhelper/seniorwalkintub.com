What to look for when comparing walk in tub models and brands

Choosing a walk in tub is a significant home investment, and the right model depends on factors that vary from household to household. Below is a plain-language guide to the key features that differentiate walk in tub models — so you can have a more informed conversation when you request a quote.

Door style: inswing vs outswing walk in tubs

Most walk in tubs use an inward-swinging door — you step in, close the door, then fill the tub. Inswing doors are generally narrower and create a tighter watertight seal. Outward-swinging walk in tub doors are available on select Ella's Bubbles and Best Bath models and allow the bather to enter without stepping over a threshold at all — which can be a meaningful safety advantage for seniors with significant mobility limitations. The tradeoff is that outswing doors require more clear floor space outside the tub. We'll help you assess which style is realistic for your bathroom layout.

Soaker vs hydrotherapy vs dual massage walk in tubs

A soaker walk in tub fills with warm water and provides the comfort of a relaxing bath — simple, quiet, effective. These are often the most affordable models and ideal for seniors who simply want a safer way to enjoy a warm bath. Hydrotherapy walk in tubs add water jets that push warm water against muscles and joints — often recommended for seniors with arthritis, chronic back pain, poor circulation or post-surgical recovery needs. Dual massage systems, available on premium Ella's Bubbles models, combine both water jets and air jets simultaneously for a more comprehensive therapeutic experience. Air jets are softer than water jets and are particularly well-suited for sensitive skin.

Bariatric and wide-body walk in tub models

Standard walk in tubs typically have an interior seat width of around 17 to 19 inches. For larger bathers, bariatric walk in tubs offer extra-wide interior dimensions — often 30 inches or more — with reinforced walls and higher weight capacities, typically rated to 400–600 lbs depending on the model. Ella's Bubbles and Best Bath both offer bariatric configurations. If the bather is on the larger side, getting the right model from the start avoids costly replacements later.

Walk in tub and shower combination models

Some seniors want the option to take a quick shower rather than always filling a full tub. Walk in tub and shower combination models include an integrated shower door panel and hand-held shower head — allowing the tub to function as both a soak station and a standing shower. Ella's Bubbles offers several well-regarded combination configurations. These models typically require a slightly wider footprint than a standard walk in tub, so bathroom dimensions matter.

Standard vs ADA walk in bathtubs

ADA-compliant walk in bathtubs are built to meet specific accessibility guidelines: grab bars at ADA-specified heights, seat height within a defined range, anti-scald protection and door threshold clearances that meet accessibility standards. Not every walk in tub sold as "handicap accessible" is truly ADA compliant — if ADA compliance is a requirement (for a grant application, a care facility, or VA documentation), let us know and we'll identify the models across our four brands that meet those standards.

Inline water heaters and fast-drain features

One of the most common concerns about walk in tubs is the fill-and-drain sequence: because you enter before filling and must wait for the tub to drain before exiting, there's potential to sit in cooling water. Inline water heaters continuously circulate warm water to maintain temperature during soaking. Fast-drain systems and dual drain configurations on select models can cut drain time significantly — from six or more minutes down to two or three. These features meaningfully improve comfort and are worth discussing when comparing models.

Walk In Tub Questions

Frequently asked questions about walk in tub models and brands.

Questions we hear most often when customers are comparing walk in tub options. Call us at 1-888-779-2284 if you don't find your answer here.

Which walk in tub brand is the best? ▾

There isn't a single "best" walk in tub brand — the right brand depends on your specific situation. Ella's Bubbles offers the most model configurations and is a great starting point for most buyers. Meditub is the top choice for buyers who want American manufacturing. Best Bath leads when barrier-free shower systems are also part of the plan. Hydrodimensions is the best fit when modern aesthetics matter. We'll help you compare based on your actual needs during a free consultation.

What's the difference between Ella's Bubbles and Meditub? ▾

Ella's Bubbles is imported and offers the widest variety of configurations, features and price points in our lineup. Meditub is manufactured in the USA (Florida) and is distinguished by its domestic production and one of the industry's strongest door seal warranties. If you prioritize American manufacturing, Meditub is the clear choice. If you want more model variety and configuration flexibility, Ella's Bubbles has more options.

Can I get a walk in tub and a roll in shower from the same provider? ▾

Yes — Best Bath specializes in exactly this scenario. We can source and install both a walk in tub and a barrier-free or roll in shower system, often coordinating a single installation visit. This is a common solution for households where one family member benefits from soaking baths and another uses a wheelchair or strongly prefers a shower. Let us know when you request a quote and we'll design a solution for both.

How do I know which walk in tub will fit in my bathroom? ▾

Most walk in tubs are designed to fit a standard 60-inch bathtub footprint (60" long × 30–32" wide), which makes replacement straightforward in most homes. However, bathroom layouts vary — door swing clearance, plumbing position and alcove dimensions all matter. When you request a quote, share your bathroom's rough measurements or photos and we'll confirm which models are a realistic fit and flag any modifications that might be needed.

What is the difference between a water jet and an air jet walk in tub? ▾

Water jets push pressurized warm water through nozzles directed at specific areas of the body — more forceful and targeted, similar to a Jacuzzi-style massage. Air jets push warm air bubbles through the floor and sides of the tub — a softer, more enveloping sensation similar to being surrounded by effervescent water. Seniors with sensitive skin often prefer air jets. Those with deep joint or muscle pain often prefer water jets. Dual massage models available through Ella's Bubbles combine both systems simultaneously.

Are walk in tubs ADA compliant? ▾

Some models across our four brands are specifically engineered to meet ADA accessibility guidelines, with grab bar placement, seat height, door threshold clearance and anti-scald protection meeting those standards. Not all walk in tubs sold as "accessible" carry true ADA compliance. If ADA compliance is required — for a VA HISA grant application, a care facility or another purpose — let us know and we'll identify the qualifying models specifically.

How long does it take to drain a walk in tub? ▾

Standard walk in tubs drain in approximately 4–7 minutes. Fast-drain models and dual-drain configurations available on select Ella's Bubbles and Best Bath models can reduce this to 2–3 minutes. Inline water heaters help maintain water temperature during the drain wait. If drain time is a specific concern, ask us to focus your quote on models with the fastest drain performance.

Can walk in tubs be installed in one day? ▾

Yes — the vast majority of walk in tub installations we coordinate are completed in a single day by our factory certified installers, working within your existing plumbing footprint. Installations involving moved plumbing lines, widened doorways or significant structural changes may take longer. We'll assess your specific situation during the quote process and give you a realistic timeline before any commitment is made.
